Admission Policies


Lakeland Christian Academy is an interdenominational school offering a challenging program of Christ-centered education. In the preschool through high school, Christian families find a safe nurturing environment where Biblical values and principles are emphasized. LCA seeks to fully prepare children spiritually, academically, physically, and socially through a strong academic program enhanced with many extracurricular opportunities -- a balance designed to enable each student to fulfill God's plan for his or her life.

Eligibility Considerations


Lakeland Christian Academy does reserve the right to select students for enrollment or re-enrollment on the basis of academic performance, compatible religious commitments and personal qualifications, including a willingness to cooperate with LCA administration and abide by its policies.

Evaluation Criteria


Students seeking admission are evaluated on the basis of their transcripts, behavior, academics, references, admission questionnaires, interviews and demonstrated ability to perform satisfactorily at Lakeland Christian Academy. LCA is not equipped with the resources required to serve students seeking to be admitted into special educational programs. No student is guaranteed admission under any circumstance. Lakeland Christian Academy is not obligated to admit any student. All admission documents, interviews, and information will be formulated to determine if the school will be able to form a partnership with the family. Testing is required for students making application for entering grades K5-high school. A student who has a history of academic, discipline, or behavior problems may be accepted on probation after administrative consultation. Test scores and the completed admissions packet will be evaluated. After a decision has been reached, the applicant will be notified by phone or by letter.

Age Requirements


  • Children entering Kindergarten must have their fifth birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
  • Students entering first grade must have their sixth birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
  • Students entering second grade must have their seventh birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
  • Students entering third grade must have their eighth birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
  • Students entering fourth grade must have their ninth birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
  • Students entering fifth grade must have their tenth birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
  • Students entering sixth grade must have their eleventh birthday on or before September 1 of the enrollment year.
Notice of Nondiscriminatory Policy as to Students


Lakeland Christian Academy admits students of any race, color, national and ethnic origin to all rights, privileges, programs and activities generally accorded or made available to students at the school. LCA does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national and ethnic origin in administration of its education policies, admissions policies, and athletic and other school administered programs.

Accreditation


Lakeland Christian Academy (LCA) is accredited with the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS) and the Association of Christian Schools International (ACSI). Headquartered in Colorado Springs, Colorado, ACSI is a ministry with the goal of being an "enabler" for the evangelical Christian community of preschools, elementary and secondary schools, and post-secondary schools.
